Executive Programme • Trusted Multi-Agent AI Workflows

The next phase of AI adoption is multi-agent workflows.

Businesses are moving beyond single AI assistants toward connected AI systems that coordinate customer enquiries, policy checks, approvals, scheduling, reporting, and operational decisions.

The opportunity is powerful — but the risk is real. When AI agents communicate with each other, businesses need trust, verification, accountability, and human oversight built into the workflow.

Key question: What happens if one agent passes wrong information to another?

One AI tool is manageable. Connected AI agents need a trust framework.

As AI moves into real operations, agents will increasingly hand work to other agents. That creates new business questions around reliability, ownership, escalation, and control.

🔗

Agents will coordinate business work

Different agents may handle enquiries, knowledge checks, internal approvals, scheduling, reporting, and follow-ups.

⚠️

Mistakes can cascade

If one agent passes incorrect context to another, the error can spread across the workflow and affect downstream decisions.

🛡️

Trust must be intentionally designed

Businesses need verification, escalation paths, human checkpoints, auditability, and clear accountability.

Example: from fragmented handoffs to coordinated agent workflows.

Participants explore practical business scenarios where multiple AI agents coordinate work while keeping safeguards and human judgement in place.

Before

Teams manually coordinate across systems.

Customer requests, policy checks, scheduling, approvals, and follow-ups happen separately, creating delays, inconsistent decisions, and missed handoffs.

After

Agents coordinate the workflow with controls.

One agent captures the request, another checks approved knowledge, another routes the next step, while sensitive decisions are escalated to a human owner.
